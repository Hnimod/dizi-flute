# Dizi Fingering Charts

## How to Read These Charts

- Dizi has 6 finger holes numbered 6 (leftmost, left hand ring finger) to 1 (rightmost, right hand index finger)
- Left hand covers holes 6, 5, 4 (ring, middle, index fingers)
- Right hand covers holes 3, 2, 1 (ring, middle, index fingers)
- ● = hole fully closed
- ○ = hole fully open
- ◐ = hole half-closed
- The blowing hole and membrane hole are not covered by fingers
- "Overblow" means increase air speed/pressure to jump to the upper octave

## Alternative Fingering: 筒音作2 (All-holes-closed = Re)

Used for playing in certain keys without changing your dizi. On a D-key dizi, this system produces notes in the key of G major.

## Cross-Fingerings for Chromatic Notes

These fingerings produce notes outside the standard major scale. They are approximate and require embouchure adjustment to tune properly.

### D-Key Dizi (筒音作5)

| Note | Jianpu | Western | Hole 6 | Hole 5 | Hole 4 | Hole 3 | Hole 2 | Hole 1 | Technique |
|------|--------|---------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| Fa natural | b3 | F4 | ● | ◐ | ○ | ○ | ○ | ○ | Half-hole 5 |
| Sharp Do | #1 | D#4 | ● | ● | ● | ○ | ○ | ◐ | Half-hole 1 |
| Sharp Re | #2 | F4 | ● | ● | ○ | ○ | ○ | ◐ | Half-hole 1 |
| Flat Ti | b7 | C4 | ● | ● | ● | ○ | ● | ○ | Fork fingering |
| Sharp Sol | #5 | A#4 | ● | ● | ● | ● | ● | ◐ | Half-hole 1, overblow |
| Flat La | b6 | Bb4 | ● | ● | ● | ● | ◐ | ○ | Half-hole 2, overblow |

**Note**: Chromatic notes on dizi are inherently approximate. Fine-tuning comes from:
- Adjusting embouchure angle
- Adjusting air pressure
- Precise degree of half-holing

## Common Trill Fingerings

Trills (颤音) are a signature dizi ornament. Rapidly alternate between the base note and the note above.

### Basic Trills

| Trill | Base Fingering | Finger to Trill | Description |
|-------|---------------|-----------------|-------------|
| 1 tr 2 | Do fingering | Trill hole 4 | Rapidly open/close hole 4 |
| 2 tr 3 | Re fingering | Trill hole 5 | Rapidly open/close hole 5 |
| 3 tr 4 | Mi fingering | Trill hole 5 + open/close hole 6 | Two-finger coordination |
| 5 tr 6 | Sol fingering (overblow) | Trill hole 1 | Rapidly open/close hole 1 |
| 6 tr 7 | La fingering (overblow) | Trill hole 2 | Rapidly open/close hole 2 |
| 1̇ tr 2̇ | High Do fingering | Trill hole 4 | Same as low register, strong air |
| 2̇ tr 3̇ | High Re fingering | Trill hole 5 | Same as low register, strong air |

### Trill Practice Tips

- Start slow and gradually increase speed
- Keep non-trilling fingers completely still
- The trilling finger should stay close to the hole (minimal lift)
- Practice each trill in isolation before using in pieces

## Quick Reference: Fingering Pattern Summary

This simplified view shows the core pattern that applies to ALL key dizis:

```
All closed          = 5  (Sol)
Open 1              = 6  (La)
Open 1,2            = 7  (Ti)
Open 1,2,3          = 1  (Do)  -- one octave up from 筒音
Open 1,2,3,4        = 2  (Re)
Open 1,2,3,4,5      = 3  (Mi)
Close 5, open 6     = 4  (Fa)  -- fork fingering

Overblow any of the above = same note, one octave higher
```

This pattern is the same regardless of which key dizi you play. Only the sounding pitches change.

## Tips for Beginners

1. **Seal the holes completely** -- even a tiny gap changes the pitch. Press with confidence.
2. **Use finger pads, not fingertips** -- the fleshy part of your finger gives a better seal on the round holes.
3. **Overblowing** -- same fingering, but a faster and more focused air stream. Think of blowing across a narrower opening. Do not simply blow harder.
4. **When switching to a different key dizi**, the fingerings stay the same -- only the sounding pitches change. This is the beauty of the jianpu system.
5. **Half-holing** is an advanced technique -- slide your finger to partially uncover the hole for chromatic and microtonal adjustments. Do not worry about this until you are comfortable with all standard fingerings.
6. **Fa (4) is the odd one out** -- it uses a fork fingering (hole 6 open, hole 5 closed). This trips up beginners. Practice the transition from Mi to Fa repeatedly.
7. **Print this chart** and keep it on your music stand until fingerings are memorized. Most players internalize the basic scale within 2-3 weeks of daily practice.
8. **Learn 筒音作5 thoroughly first** before attempting alternative fingering systems (筒音作2, 筒音作1). The alternatives will make much more sense once the primary system is second nature.
