# Dizi Care and Maintenance

## Daily Care (After Every Session)
- Wipe exterior with soft, dry cloth
- Swab interior with a thin cloth or cleaning rod to remove moisture
- Never leave moisture sitting inside — bamboo absorbs water and can crack
- Store in case, not left out on a table

## Dimo (Membrane) Management

### When to Replace
- Membrane is torn or has holes
- Membrane has become soggy or lost elasticity
- Buzzing sound has become dull or muffled instead of bright
- Membrane has come unglued on one side
- Typically every 2-8 weeks depending on playing frequency and humidity

### How to Apply Dimo
1. Remove old membrane and clean residual glue from rim
2. Cut a rectangle of dimo slightly larger than the membrane hole
3. Gently stretch the dimo to reveal grain lines running across the width
4. Apply erjiao (阿胶) glue around the hole rim (moisten the glue stick with water/saliva and rub around the rim)
5. Place membrane with grain lines PERPENDICULAR to the dizi's length
6. Create fine wrinkles PARALLEL to the dizi's length using fingertips
7. The wrinkles should be very fine and even — they create the buzzing resonance
8. Press edges down firmly to seal
9. Let dry for a few minutes before playing

### Dimo Tips
- Keep spare dimo in your case at all times
- In dry environments, breathe gently on the dimo before playing to moisten slightly
- In humid environments, dimo may become soggy — let it dry
- Beginner shortcut: tape over the membrane hole with clear tape initially to focus on tone production without dimo hassle

### Adjusting the Buzz
- More wrinkles = more buzz (brighter, more traditional sound)
- Fewer wrinkles = cleaner tone (more Western-flute-like)
- Tighter membrane = higher pitched buzz
- Looser membrane = lower pitched buzz
- The "ideal" buzz is personal preference and varies by piece style

## Bamboo Care

### Oiling
- Apply food-grade mineral oil or bore oil every 2 weeks
- Thin coat on exterior, especially around binding threads
- Can use a cloth on a rod to lightly oil the bore interior
- Do NOT over-oil — a thin sheen is sufficient
- Oil prevents cracking by keeping bamboo from drying out

### Humidity and Temperature
- Ideal storage: 40-60% relative humidity
- NEVER expose to direct sunlight or heat sources
- Avoid sudden temperature/humidity changes (don't leave in car)
- In dry climates (< 30% humidity): consider a humidifier in storage case
- In humid climates (> 70%): ensure air circulation, don't seal in airtight case
- Cold bamboo is brittle — warm up gradually with hands before playing in winter

### Cracking Prevention
- Most cracks happen from: dryness, sudden temperature change, or impact
- The thread bindings on the dizi help prevent cracking — never remove them
- If a crack appears: apply super glue (cyanoacrylate) immediately, clamp gently, let cure 24h
- Two-piece dizi (with metal joint) are less prone to cracking from moisture changes

## Storage
- Store horizontally in a breathable case
- Padded cases protect from impact
- Keep away from direct heat, sunlight, air conditioning vents
- For long-term storage: oil bamboo, replace old dimo, store in case with a small humidity packet

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

| Problem | Likely Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| No sound at all | Embouchure angle wrong | Adjust lip position, blow across not into |
| Airy/weak tone | Air stream too wide | Focus lips into smaller aperture |
| Squeaking on high notes | Too much air pressure | Back off pressure, focus air stream |
| Flat pitch | Not enough air support | More diaphragm support, check dimo isn't soggy |
| Sharp pitch | Too much air pressure | Relax, less force |
| Buzzing sounds wrong | Dimo needs adjustment | Re-wrinkle or replace dimo |
| Dimo keeps falling off | Glue not applied correctly | Clean rim, re-apply erjiao glue |
| Notes won't speak | Holes not fully covered | Check finger pad placement |
| Crack in bamboo | Dryness or temperature shock | Super glue repair, increase humidity |
