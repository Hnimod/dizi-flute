- 6 finger holes numbered 6 (leftmost, left hand) to 1 (rightmost, right hand)

**Tip**: When your tuner shows the note letter, match it to the Western Note column. If it's sharp/flat, adjust your embouchure (lip angle) or air pressure. A4 = 440 Hz is the standard tuning reference — you can check this with Re (2) in the middle register.

### Why these choices?

- **Fa (4)**: Cross-fingering `○●●○○○` is the standard. The half-hole alternative is for smooth legato in advanced passages.
- **Sol (5)**: All-closed `●●●●●●` across all three registers — same fingering, just change air pressure (gentle → overblow → very strong). Simplest to learn.
- **High Fa (4̇)**: Cross-fingering `○●○●●●` is the standard. Half-hole is trickier for beginners.
- **High Sol (5̇)**: All-closed `●●●●●●` with very strong air. The other methods are for fine intonation tuning in advanced playing.
