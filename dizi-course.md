# Dizi Flute Learning Course вҖ” Complete Beginner (No Music Background)

## Context

### Learn to Apply the Dimo (Membrane)
- Cut a rectangle of dimo slightly larger than the membrane hole
- Stretch gently to reveal grain lines running across the width
- Apply erjiao glue around the hole rim
- Place membrane with grain lines **perpendicular** to the dizi's length
- Create fine wrinkles **parallel** to the dizi's length using fingertips
- **Beginner shortcut**: tape over the membrane hole with clear tape initially to focus on tone production, switch to real dimo after 2-3 weeks

**Watch these:**

- рҹ”Қ YouTube search: `dizi dimo membrane application tutorial`

### Learn Jianpu (Numbered Musical Notation)
This is how all Chinese music is written. It's simpler than Western staff notation:

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 | Do Re Mi Fa Sol La Ti |
| Dot above number | One octave higher |
| Dot below number | One octave lower |
| Plain number | Quarter note (1 beat) |
| `5 -` | Half note (2 beats) |
| `5 - - -` | Whole note (4 beats) |
| Underline below | Eighth note (half beat) |
| Double underline | Sixteenth note (quarter beat) |
| 0 | Rest |
| `1=D` | Key signature (Do = D) |

**Learn jianpu from:**

## Phase 1: First Sound (Weeks 1-4)

### Week 1-2: Making a Tone
**Goal**: Produce a steady tone on at least one note

**Practice routine (20 min/day)**:
1. **Bottle exercise (5 min)**: Blow across an empty glass bottle to feel the air-splitting concept
2. **Single note attempts (15 min)**: Cover all 6 holes, place blowing hole against lower lip (covering 1/4 of the hole), blow gently across вҖ” NOT into вҖ” the far edge

**Embouchure tips**:
- Say "pu" вҖ” that lip shape is your starting point
- Think "cooling hot soup" вҖ” gentle, focused air stream
- Relax everything: shoulders, jaw, fingers, stomach
- Practice in front of a mirror to check lip position
- If no sound: adjust angle (tilt dizi toward/away from you), adjust lip coverage, make aperture smaller

- рҹ”Қ YouTube search: `dizi embouchure first sound beginner tutorial`
- рҹ”Қ YouTube search: `how to play dizi first time beginner`

**Milestone**: Can produce a tone on demand (even if inconsistent)

### Week 3-4: First Notes & Long Tones
**Goal**: Play notes 1-5 (Do to Sol) with recognizable pitch

**Practice routine (25 min/day)**:
1. **Long tones (10 min)**: Hold each note as long as possible (aim for 8+ seconds). Focus on steady, even tone. Start with all-holes-closed (low 5), then open holes one at a time
2. **Fingering practice (10 min)**: Learn and drill these fingerings:

**D-Key Dizi Fingering (Holes: 6 5 4 | 3 2 1)**:
| Note | Jianpu | 6 | 5 | 4 | 3 | 2 | 1 |
|------|--------|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| D (Do) | 1 | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ӣ | в—Ӣ | в—Ӣ |
| E (Re) | 2 | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ӣ | в—Ӣ | в—Ӣ | в—Ӣ |
| F# (Mi) | 3 | в—Ҹ | в—Ӣ | в—Ӣ | в—Ӣ | в—Ӣ | в—Ӣ |
| G (Fa) | 4 | в—Ӣ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ӣ | в—Ӣ | в—Ӣ |
| A (Sol) | 5 | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ (overblow) |

(в—Ҹ = closed, в—Ӣ = open)

3. **Scale walk (5 min)**: Play 1-2-3-4-5 slowly, one note per breath

## Phase 2: Basic Scale & First Song (Weeks 5-8)

### Week 5-6: Complete First Octave
**Goal**: Play the full scale 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 and begin second octave

**Additional fingerings**:
| Note | Jianpu | 6 | 5 | 4 | 3 | 2 | 1 |
|------|--------|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Low A (low 5) | 5МЈ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ |
| Low B (low 6) | 6МЈ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ӣ |
| Low C# (low 7) | 7МЈ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ӣ | в—Ӣ |
| B (La) | 6 | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ӣ (overblow) |

| High D | 1МҮ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ҹ | в—Ӣ | в—Ӣ | в—Ӣ (overblow) |

**Practice routine (30 min/day)**:
1. **Long tones (10 min)**: All notes you know, hold each 8-10 seconds
2. **Scale up and down (5 min)**: 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 1МҮ вҖ” 1МҮ 7 6 5 4 3 2 1
3. **Pentatonic scale (5 min)**: 1 2 3 5 6 (skip 4 and 7) вҖ” this is the foundation of Chinese music
4. **Tonguing intro (5 min)**: Say "tu" silently while blowing to articulate notes. Practice: tu-tu-tu-tu on a single note
5. **Simple melody (5 min)**: Begin learning **е°Ҹжҳҹжҳҹ (Twinkle Twinkle Little Star)**:
   `1 1 5 5 | 6 6 5 - | 4 4 3 3 | 2 2 1 - |`

## Phase 3: Building Repertoire (Months 3-4)

### Month 3: Folk Songs & Tone Quality
**Goal**: Play 3-4 songs, improve tone consistency

**Practice routine (30 min/day)**:
1. **Long tones (8 min)**: Include dynamics вҖ” start soft, grow loud, return to soft on each note
2. **Scale exercises (7 min)**: Major scale + pentatonic, ascending and descending, varying rhythm
3. **Tonguing (5 min)**: Single tongue (tu) on scale passages. Speed gradually increasing.
4. **Songs (10 min)**:
   - **иҢүиҺүиҠұ (Mo Li Hua / Jasmine Flower)** вҖ” the most famous Chinese folk song, pentatonic, beautiful
   - **еә·е®ҡжғ…жӯҢ (Kangding Love Song)** вҖ” Sichuan folk song
   - **дё–дёҠеҸӘжңүеҰҲеҰҲеҘҪ (Only Mama is Good)**

**New concept вҖ” The Chinese Pentatonic Scale**:
Five notes: Gong (е®«), Shang (е•Ҷ), Jue (и§’), Zhi (еҫө), Yu (зҫҪ) = 1 2 3 5 6
This 2,500-year-old system is the backbone of Chinese music. Most songs you'll learn use primarily these 5 notes.

### Month 4: Second Octave Confidence
**Goal**: Play comfortably in both octaves, begin simple ornaments

**Practice routine (30 min/day)**:

3. **Grace notes intro (5 min)**: Quick flick of a finger before a main note (e.g., briefly touch and release hole 5 before playing note 1). This adds the characteristic "Chinese" flavor.
4. Songs (10 min):

   - **Scarborough Fair** (works beautifully on dizi)
   - Review and polish иҢүиҺүиҠұ

## Phase 4: Expression & Technique (Months 5-7)

### Month 5: Vibrato & Dynamics
**Goal**: Add expression to playing through vibrato and dynamic control

**New techniques**:
- **Breath vibrato (ж°”йңҮйҹі)**: Pulse your diaphragm gently while sustaining a note, creating a wavering pitch. Start slow (2-3 pulses/sec), speed up gradually. This is the signature expressive technique of dizi.
- **Finger vibrato (жҢҮйўӨйҹі)**: Rapidly tap a finger near a hole (not fully closing it) to create pitch oscillation.
- **Dynamics**: Practice playing the same note pp (very soft) to ff (very loud) and back.

**Practice routine (35 min/day)**:
1. Long tones with vibrato (8 min)
2. Dynamic exercises: crescendo/diminuendo on each note (5 min)
3. Scale runs with tonguing (7 min)
4. Ornament drills: grace notes, trills (5 min)
5. Songs with expression (10 min):
   - Revisit иҢүиҺүиҠұ вҖ” now add vibrato on long notes and grace notes
   - **йҫҷзҡ„дј дәә (Descendants of the Dragon)**

**Watch & reference:**
- рҹ”Қ YouTube search: `dizi vibrato ж°”йңҮйҹі tutorial`
- рҹ”Қ YouTube search: `dizi finger vibrato жҢҮйўӨйҹі technique`
- рҹ”Қ YouTube search: `dizi йҫҷзҡ„дј дәә tutorial`
- рҹ“– Sound of Dragon вҖ” Dizi techniques: https://soundofdragon.com/dizi/

### Month 6: Double Tonguing
**Goal**: Learn double tonguing for faster passages

**Double tonguing (еҸҢеҗҗ)**: Alternate "tu-ku-tu-ku" to tongue faster than single tonguing allows.
- Practice "ku" alone first вҖ” this is the weak syllable
- Then alternate: tu-ku-tu-ku on a single note at 60 BPM, increase gradually
- Apply to scale passages

**Practice routine (35 min/day)**:
1. Long tones + vibrato (7 min)
2. Double tonguing drills (8 min)
3. Scales in various rhythms and articulations (5 min)
4. Ornaments: trills, grace notes, slides (5 min)
5. Songs (10 min):
   - Begin **Grade 3 exam pieces** from CCOM books
   - Folk song medleys

**Watch & reference:**
- рҹ”Қ YouTube search: `dizi еҚ•еҗҗ еҸҢеҗҗ tonguing technique tutorial`
- рҹ”Қ YouTube search: `dizi double tonguing tu ku practice`
- рҹ“– Triple tonguing guide: https://www.flutenotation.com/blog/tripletonguing1
- рҹ“– CCOM Exam Books (Grades 1-6): https://eight-tones.store/products/dizi-grading-examination-book-by-ccom-nafa-beginner-grade-1-6/

### Month 7: Slides & Ornamental Vocabulary
**Goal**: Develop the ornamental vocabulary that makes dizi playing authentic

**New techniques**:
- **Slides (ж»‘йҹі)**: Gradually slide finger off/onto a hole for portamento effect
- **Trills (йўӨйҹі)**: Rapid alternation between two adjacent notes
- **Flutter tongue (иҠұиҲҢ)**: Roll your tongue (like a rolled R) while blowing вҖ” creates a tremolo effect

**Watch & reference:**
- рҹ”Қ YouTube search: `dizi slide technique ж»‘йҹі tutorial`

**Milestone**: Your playing sounds recognizably "dizi-like" with characteristic Chinese ornaments. Can play 8-10 pieces with expression. Ready for CCOM Grade 2-3 material.

## Key Principles

1. **Consistency > Duration**: 30 min daily beats 3 hours on weekends
2. **Slow practice is fast learning**: Always learn passages slowly first, speed up gradually with metronome
3. **Record yourself weekly**: You can't hear yourself accurately while playing
4. **Long tones are non-negotiable**: Do them every session, forever
5. **Dimo management**: Replace membrane every few weeks. Carry spares always.
6. **Relax**: Tension in shoulders, jaw, fingers, stomach = poor tone and fatigue
7. **Use finger pads, not tips**: Fleshy part of finger covers holes, not bony fingertips
8. **Blow across, not into**: Air stream splits across far edge of blowing hole

---

## Dizi Maintenance

- **After every session**: Wipe exterior dry, swab interior with cloth
- **Never wash with water** вҖ” bamboo will crack
- **Oil bamboo every 2 weeks** with food-grade mineral oil (thin coat)
- **Store horizontally** in breathable case, 40-60% humidity
- **Replace dimo** when it tears, gets soggy, or loses its buzz (every few weeks to months)

## Verification / How to Track Progress

- **Week 4**: Can produce a tone on demand and play notes 1-5
- **Week 8**: Can play 2 simple songs from memory (е°Ҹжҳҹжҳҹ, е°ҸзҷҪиҸң)
- **Month 4**: Can play 5+ songs with both octaves. Beginning ornaments.
- **Month 7**: Playing sounds authentically "Chinese" with vibrato and ornaments. 8-10 pieces.
- **Month 10**: 2-3 performance-ready pieces. Comfortable with CCOM Grade 3-4.
- **Month 12**: 15+ pieces. Solid fundamentals. Ready for intermediate repertoire.
