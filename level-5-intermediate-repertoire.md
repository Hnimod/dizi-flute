# Level 5: Intermediate Repertoire

**Timeline:** Months 8-12
**CCOM Grade:** Grade 3-5
**Goal:** Tackle recognized dizi repertoire, understand northern/southern styles, develop performance skills

---

## Music Theory for This Level

### Mixed Meter
- Some pieces change time signatures within the piece (e.g., 4/4 → 2/4 → 4/4)
- Follow the time signature changes as written

### Rubato and 散板 (Sanban — Free Time)
- 散板 = "scattered beat" — no fixed tempo, performer plays freely
- Very common in Chinese music, especially at beginnings and endings of pieces
- Marked as 散板 at the start of a section
- The performer stretches and compresses time for expression
- This is one of the most important concepts in Chinese music

### Cadenza-Like Passages (华彩段)
- Extended solo passages where the player shows virtuosity
- Often in free time
- Common in grade 4+ pieces

### Traditional Chinese Piece Structure
Many traditional pieces follow this pattern:
- **散 (san)** — Free time introduction, sets the mood
- **慢 (man)** — Slow section, lyrical melody
- **中 (zhong)** — Medium tempo, development
- **快 (kuai)** — Fast section, climax/excitement
- **散 (san)** — Free time conclusion, return to calm

This 散-慢-中-快-散 structure is THE classical form of Chinese instrumental music. 姑苏行 follows this pattern.

### Northern vs Southern Dizi Styles

| Aspect | Northern (北派) | Southern (南派) |
|--------|----------------|----------------|
| Instrument | Bangdi (梆笛) — G, A key | Qudi (曲笛) — C, D, E key |
| Character | Bright, energetic, percussive | Mellow, lyrical, flowing |
| Techniques | Double/triple tonguing, 花舌 (flutter tongue), sharp staccato | Vibrato, slides, legato, breath control |
| Ornaments | 吐音 (tonguing) dominant | 气震音 (breath vibrato) dominant |
| Repertoire | 喜相逢, 五梆子, 扬鞭催马运粮忙 | 姑苏行, 鹧鸪飞, 牧笛 |
| Regions | Northeast, North China, Northwest | Jiangnan, South China |
| Emotion | Joy, celebration, humor, vigor | Elegance, longing, scenery, introspection |

### Modulation (转调)
- Some pieces change key mid-stream
- In jianpu: shown as a new key signature (e.g., 1=D changes to 1=G)
- On dizi: may require switching instrument or using alternative fingering (筒音作2)

### Ensemble Basics: Heterophony (支声)
- In Chinese ensemble music, instruments don't play in Western-style harmony
- Instead they play the same melody with individual ornamental variations — this is called heterophony (支声)
- Each instrument adds its own ornaments and slight rhythmic variations to a shared melody

## New Techniques

### Triple Tonguing (三吐)
- Pattern 1: tu-tu-ku (for groups of 3)
- Pattern 2: tu-ku-tu (alternative)
- Used for triplet passages and very fast runs
- Practice on single note first, then scales

### Refined Slides (滑音)
- 上滑音 (upward slide): gradually reveal hole, pitch rises to target
- 下滑音 (downward slide): gradually cover hole, pitch falls to target
- Speed of slide = expressive choice (fast slide vs slow portamento)
- Southern style uses many slides

### Circular Breathing (循环换气) — Introduction
- The legendary technique: inhale through nose while blowing air from cheeks
- Steps:
  1. Puff cheeks full of air
  2. While squeezing cheek air out, inhale quickly through nose
  3. Resume normal blowing seamlessly
- Practice without dizi first: blow through a straw into water, maintain bubbles while breathing in through nose
- This takes weeks/months to develop — start now, mastery comes in Level 6-7

### Performance Skills
- Musical phrasing: where to breathe, where to push/pull tempo
- Consistent tone across all registers
- Clean ornaments at tempo
- Record yourself weekly and critically listen back
- Stage presence basics: confidence, communication, eye contact (for recitals)

## Practice Routine (40 min/day)

1. Warm-up: long tones + vibrato + scales (8 min)
2. Technical exercises: tonguing (single/double/triple), ornaments, intervals (10 min)
3. Etude or exercise piece (7 min)
4. Repertoire — work on ONE piece deeply (15 min)

## Exercises in Jianpu

### Exercise 1: Triple Tonguing Drill
```
1=D  2/4
tu-tu-ku pattern on triplets:

1 1 1  2 2 2 | 3 3 3  5 5 5 | 6 6 6  5 5 5 | 3 3 3  2 2 2 |
1 1 1  1 - ||
```

### Exercise 2: Slide Practice
```
1=D  4/4
↗ = upward slide into note, ↘ = downward slide

1 - ↗3 - | 3 - ↗5 - | 5 - ↗6 - | 6 - ↘5 - |
5 - ↘3 - | 3 - ↘1 - ||
```

### Exercise 3: 散板 Free Time Exercise
```
1=D  散板
Play freely, stretch and compress time:

5̣ - - 6̣ 1 | 2 3 - - - | 5 - 6 5 3 | 2 - - - |
1 - - - 6̣ | 5̣ - - - - ||
```
No metronome. Feel each note. Breathe naturally.

## Songs in Jianpu

### Song 1: 姑苏行 (A Walk in Gusu / Gusu Xing)

THE milestone piece. Grade 5, southern style masterpiece by Jiang Xianwei (1962). Depicts a morning stroll through the ancient city of Suzhou. This piece uses the 散-慢-中-快-散 structure.

**Section A: 散板 Introduction (Free time)**
```
姑苏行 (A Walk in Gusu) — Introduction
1=D  散板

0 0 0 5̣ 6̣ | 1 2 3 5 3 | 2 - - 1 2 |
3 5 6 1̇ 6 | 5 - - 3 5 |
6 5 3 2 | 1 - 6̣ 5̣ | 6̣ - - - ||
```

**Section B: 慢板 Slow melody**
```
1=D  4/4
Adagio ♩=60

6̣ 1 2 3 | 5 - 3 2 | 1 - - 6̣ | 5̣ - - 0 |
6̣ 1 2 3 | 5 - 6 5 | 3 - 2 1 | 2 - - 0 |
3 5 6 1̇ | 6 - 5 3 | 5 - 3 2 | 1 - - 0 |
6̣ 1 2 3 | 2 - 1 6̣ | 5̣ - - - | 0 0 0 0 ||
```
Add: rich vibrato on held notes, 叠音 grace notes, gentle slides.

**Section C: 中板-快板 Medium to fast**
```
1=D  2/4
Allegro ♩=132

6̣ 1 | 2 3 | 5 3 | 2 1 |
6̣ 1 | 2 3 | 5 6 | 5 3 |
2 3 | 5 6 | 1̇ 6 | 5 3 |
2 1 | 6̣ 5̣ | 6̣ 1 | 2 - ||
```

**Note:** This is a simplified teaching version. The full piece is longer and more ornamented. Work on the complete version from the CCOM exam book once you've mastered these sections.

### Song 2: 小放牛 (Little Cowherd)
Grade 3-4, northern style. Playful and lively, based on a Hebei folk melody.

```
小放牛 (Little Cowherd)
1=D  2/4
Allegretto ♩=120

5 6 | 1̇ 6 5 3 | 5 - | 3 2 1 2 |
3 - | 5 6 1̇ 6 | 5 3 2 1 | 6̣ - |
5̣ 6̣ | 1 2 3 5 | 3 2 1 6̣ | 5̣ - |
6̣ 1 | 2 3 5 3 | 2 1 6̣ 5̣ | 6̣ - ||
```
Use double tonguing in fast passages. Bright, energetic character.

### Song 3: 牧笛 (Shepherd's Flute)
A lyrical piece by Liu Zhi (1958). Tests tone quality and breath control.

```
牧笛 (Shepherd's Flute)
1=D  4/4
Andante ♩=66

0 0 5̣ 6̣ | 1 - 2 3 | 5 3 2 - | 1 2 3 5 |
6 - - 5 | 3 2 1 6̣ | 5̣ - - - | 0 0 5̣ 6̣ |
1 - 6̣ 1 | 2 3 5 3 | 2 - 1 2 | 3 5 6 - |
5 3 2 1 | 6̣ 5̣ 6̣ 1 | 2 - 1 6̣ | 5̣ - - - ||
```

### Song 4: 喜相逢 (Happy Reunion)
Grade 4-5, northern style by Feng Zicun. Energetic, showcases double tonguing. Based on a Inner Mongolian folk melody depicting the joy of friends reuniting.

```
喜相逢 (Happy Reunion) — Slow Section
1=D  4/4
Adagio ♩=52

5̣ - 6̣ 1 | 2 - 1 6̣ | 5̣ - - - | 6̣ - 1 2 |
3 - 2 1 | 6̣ 5̣ 6̣ - | 5̣ - - - | 1 - 2 3 |
5 - 3 2 | 1 - 6̣ 5̣ | 6̣ 1 2 - | 1 - - - ||

喜相逢 — Fast Section
1=D  2/4
Allegro ♩=144

5̣ 6̣ 1 2 | 3 2 1 6̣ | 5̣ 6̣ 1 2 | 3 5 6 5 |
3 2 1 6̣ | 5̣ 6̣ 1 2 | 3 5 3 2 | 1 6̣ 5̣ - ||
```
Double tongue throughout the fast section.

### Song 5: 扬鞭催马运粮忙 (Whipping the Horse to Transport Grain)

A traditional Jiangnan silk and bamboo (江南丝竹) ensemble piece. Elegant southern style.

## Buying Your Second Dizi

At this level, consider buying a second instrument:
- **G-key bangdi (梆笛)**: For northern-style pieces (smaller, higher, brighter)
- **C-key qudi (曲笛)**: For deeper southern pieces (larger, lower, mellower)
- **E or F-key**: Versatile middle options

See: [Eason — What Keys Should an Amateur Get?](https://www.easonmusicschool.com/what-keys-of-dizi-should-an-amateur-get/)

## Performance Preparation

### How to Prepare a Piece for Performance
1. **Learn notes slowly** — no tempo, just accuracy
2. **Add rhythm** — with metronome, still slow
3. **Add expression** — dynamics, vibrato, ornaments
4. **Bring to tempo** — gradually increase metronome speed
5. **Memorize** — play without looking at jianpu
6. **Record and critique** — listen back, note problems
7. **Polish** — fix issues, refine details
8. **Perform** — for friends, family, or recording

### Recording Tips
- Record weekly with your phone camera
- Compare Month 1 recordings with Month 10
- Listen for: tone quality, intonation, rhythm accuracy, expression
- Use **Tonal Energy** app to check intonation

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| 散板 sounds random, not musical | Listen to master recordings many times. Feel the natural flow of the melody. |
| Fast passages are messy | Slow down drastically, then gradually increase speed with metronome |
| Northern style sounds flat/dead | More tonguing energy, sharper staccato, brighter tone |
| Southern style sounds plain | More vibrato, more slides, warmer dynamics, singing tone |
| Circular breathing breaks the sound | Keep practicing the straw-in-water exercise. Patience required. |
| Can't memorize long pieces | Break into 4-bar chunks. Memorize one chunk at a time. Link them. |

## Checklist Before Moving to Level 6

- [ ] Can play 12-15 pieces total
- [ ] 2-3 pieces are "performance ready" (memorized, expressive, at tempo)
- [ ] Understand northern vs southern styles and can identify them by ear
- [ ] Triple tonguing functional at moderate speed
- [ ] Beginning circular breathing attempts
- [ ] Can play in 散板 (free time) musically
- [ ] Comfortable with CCOM Grade 3-5 material
- [ ] Own or considering a second dizi in a different key
