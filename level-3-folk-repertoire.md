# Level 3: Folk Repertoire

**Timeline:** Months 3-4
**CCOM Grade:** Grade 1-2
**Goal:** Play 5+ folk songs with both octaves, begin adding expression through dynamics and grace notes

---

## Music Theory for This Level

### 3/4 Time Signature

Three beats per measure, with emphasis on beat 1: **ONE** two three **ONE** two three.

This creates a waltz-like feel. Count: `1 2 3 | 1 2 3 |`

You'll encounter this in Scarborough Fair and 新年好 (if you played it in Level 2).

### Dotted Notes (附点音符)

A dot after a note extends it by **half its original value**:

| Notation | Original | Dot adds | Total |
|----------|----------|----------|-------|
| `5.` | 1 beat (quarter) | + ½ beat | 1.5 beats |
| `5 - .` | 2 beats (half) | + 1 beat | 3 beats |
| `5 - - - .` | 4 beats (whole) | + 2 beats | 6 beats |

Dotted rhythms create a "long-short" swing feel: `5. 6` = Sol (1.5 beats) La (0.5 beats).

### Ties and Slurs (延音线 and 连音线)

Both are shown as a curved arc ⌒ above or below notes:

- **Tie** (延音线): Connects two notes of the **same pitch** — hold through without re-tonguing
  - `5 ⌒ 5` = one long Sol (2 beats total)
- **Slur** (连音线): Connects **different** notes — play smoothly, tongue only the first note
  - `5 ⌒ 6` = Sol flows into La without tonguing La

### Dynamics (力度记号)

Dynamics tell you how loud or soft to play:

| Symbol | Italian | Chinese | Meaning |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| pp | pianissimo | 最弱 | Very soft |
| p | piano | 弱 | Soft |
| mp | mezzo piano | 中弱 | Moderately soft |
| mf | mezzo forte | 中强 | Moderately loud |
| f | forte | 强 | Loud |
| ff | fortissimo | 最强 | Very loud |
| < | crescendo | 渐强 | Gradually louder |
| > | diminuendo | 渐弱 | Gradually softer |

**How to control dynamics on dizi:**
- **Louder**: More air support from diaphragm (not just blowing harder)
- **Softer**: Less air, slightly adjusted embouchure
- Practice: play one note starting pp, crescendo to ff, then diminuendo back to pp

### The Heptatonic Scale — Qi Sheng (七声)

The pentatonic scale (Level 2) has 5 notes: 1 2 3 5 6. The **heptatonic** (7-note) scale adds notes 4 and 7 back:

`1 2 3 4 5 6 7`

Chinese music theory recognizes three types of heptatonic scales:

| Type | Chinese | Notes | Character |
|------|---------|-------|-----------|
| Qingyue (清乐) | 清乐音阶 | 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 | Bright, common in folk |
| Yayue (雅乐) | 雅乐音阶 | 1 2 3 #4 5 6 7 | Elegant, ancient court music |
| Yanyue (燕乐) | 燕乐音阶 | 1 2 3 4 5 6 b7 | Festive, banquet music |

For now, most songs you play use the standard qingyue scale or pure pentatonic. The other modes become important at Level 6.

### Musical Form: AB Structure

The simplest song structure has two sections:
- **A section**: The main melody (verse)
- **B section**: A contrasting melody (chorus or bridge)

Many folk songs are **through-composed** (no repeated sections) — the melody flows from beginning to end. 茉莉花 is a good example.

### Tempo Markings

| Western | BPM | Chinese | Feel |
|---------|-----|---------|------|
| Adagio | 60-76 | 慢板 | Slow, expressive |
| Andante | 76-108 | 行板 | Walking pace |
| Moderato | 108-120 | 中板 | Moderate |
| Allegro | 120-156 | 快板 | Fast, lively |

## New Technique: Grace Notes (装饰音)

Grace notes are quick, ornamental notes played just before the main note. They add the characteristic "Chinese" flavor to dizi playing.

**How to play:** Briefly and quickly finger a neighboring note before landing on the target note. The grace note is so fast it barely has duration — it's more of a "flick."

**In jianpu:** Grace notes are written as small numbers before the main note, or marked with a small curve.

**Types you'll use now:**
- **Upper grace note**: Quickly finger one note above, then land on the main note
  - Example: grace 6 before 5 = quickly play La then land on Sol
- **Lower grace note**: Quickly finger one note below, then land
  - Example: grace 2 before 3 = quickly play Re then land on Mi

**Exercise:** Play a slow scale, adding a grace note before each note:
```
1=D  4/4

(2)3 - - - | (1)2 - - - | (5)6 - - - | (6)5 - - - ||
```
(Numbers in parentheses are grace notes — play them very quickly before the main note)

## Practice Routine (30 min/day)

1. **Long tones with dynamics (8 min)** — each note pp→ff→pp
2. **Scales (7 min)** — major scale + pentatonic, ascending/descending, varying rhythms
3. **Grace note drills (5 min)** — add grace notes to scale passages
4. **Song practice (10 min)** — work through songs below

## Songs in Jianpu

### Song 1: 茉莉花 (Jasmine Flower) — Jiangsu Version

The most famous Chinese folk song. Pentatonic, lyrical, beautiful. This is a milestone piece — take your time to learn it well.

```
茉莉花 (Jasmine Flower)
1=D  2/4
Andante ♩=72

3 3 | 5 6̇ 1̇ 6 | 5 - | 5 6̇ 1̇ 6 |
5 - | 5 3 | 5 6 5 3 | 2 - |
1 6̣ | 5̣ 6̣ 1 - | 2 3 | 2 1 6̣ 5̣ |
6̣ 1 | 2 - | 3 5 | 3 2 1 2 |
3 - | 5 3 | 2 3 5 3 | 2 - |
1 6̣ | 1 5̣ | 6̣ - ||
```

**Practice tips:**
- Learn it phrase by phrase (2 bars at a time)
- The melody uses only pentatonic notes (1 2 3 5 6)
- Keep the tempo slow and flowing — this is a lyrical song
- Add grace notes on repeated notes once you're comfortable with the melody

### Song 2: 康定情歌 (Kangding Love Song)

A famous Sichuan folk song about love. Lively and joyful.

```
康定情歌 (Kangding Love Song)
1=G  2/4
Moderato ♩=108

5 5̇ | 6̇ 5̇ | 3 5 | 6 1̇ | 5 - |
3 2 | 3 5 - | 2 1 | 6̣ 1 | 2 - |
5 5̇ | 6̇ 5̇ | 3 5 | 6 1̇ | 5 - |
3 2 | 1 6̣ | 5̣ - - ||
```
*Note: Written in 1=G. On a D-key dizi, use 筒音作2 fingering, or simply play with D-key fingerings for practice.*

### Song 3: 南泥湾 (Nanniwan)

A patriotic folk song from the 1940s. Warm and uplifting melody.

```
南泥湾 (Nanniwan)
1=D  2/4
Moderato ♩=100

0 5 3 | 5 6 | 1̇ 6 | 5 - | 0 5 3 | 5 6 | 5 - |
3 5 | 6 5 | 3 2 | 1 - | 6̣ 1 | 2 3 | 2 - |
0 5 3 | 5 6 | 1̇ 6 | 5 - | 0 5 3 | 5 6 | 5 - |
3 2 | 1 6̣ | 5̣ 6̣ | 1 - - ||
```

### Song 4: Scarborough Fair

An English folk ballad that sounds gorgeous on dizi. In 3/4 time.

```
Scarborough Fair
1=D  3/4
Andante ♩=80

6̣ - 6̣ | 3 - 2 | 3 - 1 | 6̣ - - |
0 - 6̣ | 5 - 6 | 3 - - | 3 - - |
0 - 6 | 6 - 5 | 3 - 2 | 3 - 1 |
6̣ - 1 | 2 - 1 | 6̣ - - | 6̣ - - ||
```

**Note:** This uses 3/4 time (waltz feel). Count: ONE two three | ONE two three.

### Song 5: 送别 (Farewell)

Based on the melody of "Dreaming of Home and Mother" by J.P. Ordway. One of China's most cherished songs, famously used in the film "城南旧事."

```
送别 (Farewell)
1=D  4/4
Andante ♩=72

5 3 5 | 1̇ - - | 6 1̇ 6 | 5 - - |
5 6̣ 1 | 2 - 1 | 2 - - | 0 - - |
5 3 5 | 1̇ - 7 | 6 1̇ 6 | 5 - - |
5 2 3 | 1 - - | 1 - - | 0 - - ||
```

### Song 6: 小燕子 (Little Swallow)

A classic Chinese children's song from the 1950s film. Very singable melody.

```
小燕子 (Little Swallow)
1=D  2/4

3 5 | 5 - | 3 5 | 1̇ - |
6 1̇ | 6 5 | 3 1 | 2 - |
2 3 | 4 3 | 2 - | 5 3 |
4 2 | 1 - ||
```

### Song 7: 彝族舞曲 (Yi Dance Melody — Simplified)

A simplified version of a Yi ethnic minority melody. Great introduction to ethnic Chinese music.

```
彝族舞曲 (Yi Dance — Simplified)
1=D  3/4

3 2 1 | 6̣ - - | 1 2 3 | 2 - - |
3 5 6 | 5 3 2 | 1 6̣ 1 | 2 - - |
3 2 1 | 6̣ - 5̣ | 6̣ 1 2 | 1 - - ||
```

## The Chinese Pentatonic Modes (Preview)

You've learned that the pentatonic scale is 1 2 3 5 6. But any of these notes can serve as the "home" note (tonic), creating different modes:

| Mode | Tonic | Feel | Common in |
|------|-------|------|-----------|
| Gong (宫) | 1 | Bright, major | Most folk songs |
| Shang (商) | 2 | Open, floating | Northwest folk |
| Jue (角) | 3 | Gentle, minor | Jiangnan silk/bamboo |
| Zhi (徵) | 5 | Strong, martial | Northern folk |
| Yu (羽) | 6 | Melancholic, minor | Sorrow songs |

Most songs you've learned so far are in Gong mode (home note = 1) or Yu mode (home note = 6, like 小白菜). You'll explore modes deeply at Level 6.

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Grace notes are too slow/heavy | They should be instantaneous — flick the finger, don't "play" the grace note |
| Dynamics are hard to control | Practice long tones at each dynamic level separately before crescendos |
| 茉莉花 is hard to memorize | Learn 2 bars at a time. Sing it first. Listen to recordings repeatedly. |
| Tone breaks when changing octaves | Practice the octave transition notes (5̣→5, 6̣→6) slowly and repeatedly |
| Running out of breath in long phrases | Plan breathing points. Quick inhale through mouth corners without removing dizi. |
