## Music Theory for This Level

### Irregular Meters
- 5/4 time: 5 beats per measure (often grouped 3+2 or 2+3)
- 7/8 time: 7 eighth notes per measure (often 2+2+3 or 3+2+2)
- Found in folk music from Xinjiang, Tibet, and other ethnic minorities

### Comprehensive Chinese Modal Theory — The Five Pentatonic Modes

Each of the 5 pentatonic notes can serve as the tonic (home note), creating 5 distinct modes. This is fundamental to understanding Chinese music at a deep level.

| Mode | Chinese | Tonic | Scale | Character | Common In | Example |
|------|---------|-------|-------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Gong | 宫调式 | 1 (Do) | 1 2 3 5 6 | Bright, stable, major feel | Most standard folk songs | 茉莉花 |
| Shang | 商调式 | 2 (Re) | 2 3 5 6 1̇ | Open, spacious, floating | Northwest folk music (Shaanxi, Gansu) | Qinqiang opera melodies |
| Jue | 角调式 | 3 (Mi) | 3 5 6 1̇ 2̇ | Gentle, lyrical, tender | Jiangnan silk and bamboo music | Relatively rare as pure mode |
| Zhi | 徵调式 | 5 (Sol) | 5 6 1̇ 2̇ 3̇ | Strong, warm, open | Northern folk, patriotic songs | Many revolutionary songs |
| Yu | 羽调式 | 6 (La) | 6 1̇ 2̇ 3̇ 5̇ | Melancholic, introspective, minor feel | Sorrowful songs, lyrical pieces | 小白菜 |

**Gong Mode (宫调式)** — Tonic = 1 (Do)
- Scale: 1 2 3 5 6
- Character: Bright, stable, major feel
- Common in: Most standard folk songs
- Example pieces: 茉莉花
- The most "default" sounding mode — feels resolved and settled on 1

**Shang Mode (商调式)** — Tonic = 2 (Re)
- Scale: 2 3 5 6 1̇
- Character: Open, spacious, floating
- Common in: Northwest folk music (Shaanxi, Gansu)
- Example: some Qinqiang opera melodies
- Has a distinctive "northwestern" quality — wide and windswept

**Jue Mode (角调式)** — Tonic = 3 (Mi)
- Scale: 3 5 6 1̇ 2̇
- Character: Gentle, lyrical, tender
- Common in: Jiangnan silk and bamboo music
- Relatively rare as a pure mode
- Soft and understated — the most subtle of the five modes

**Zhi Mode (徵调式)** — Tonic = 5 (Sol)
- Scale: 5 6 1̇ 2̇ 3̇
- Character: Strong, warm, open
- Common in: Northern folk, patriotic songs
- Example pieces: Many revolutionary songs
- Bold and outward-facing — conveys strength and optimism

**Yu Mode (羽调式)** — Tonic = 6 (La)
- Scale: 6 1̇ 2̇ 3̇ 5̇
- Character: Melancholic, introspective, minor feel
- Common in: Sorrowful songs, lyrical pieces
- Example: 小白菜 is in Yu mode
- The "natural minor" equivalent — deeply emotional and inward-looking

### Regional Scales

**Xinjiang Scales:** Use augmented 2nds (e.g., 1 2 b3 #4 5 6 b7) — Middle Eastern influence. The augmented 2nd interval (b3 to #4) gives Xinjiang music its instantly recognizable exotic character. Uyghur, Kazakh, and Tajik music all use variations of these scales.

**Mongolian Long Song Scale:** Extended pentatonic with wide intervals, reflecting vast grasslands. Melodies often span a wide range with large leaps, and sustained notes with rich vibrato evoke the open steppe. The dizi piece 牧民新歌 draws on this tradition.

**Cantonese Scale:** Uses special intervals, particularly the Cantonese "raised 4th" (between 4 and #4). This gives Cantonese opera and Guangdong music its distinctive flavor. The scale also features a "neutral 7th" — between b7 and 7.

### Musical Form: Rondo and Theme & Variations
- **Rondo (ABACA)**: Main theme keeps returning between contrasting episodes. Each return of A may be varied or ornamented differently.
- **Theme and Variations**: A melody is stated then transformed in successive variations (very common in Chinese instrumental music — e.g., the "liu ban" 六板 structure where the same melody is progressively ornamented and developed). Each variation adds more notes, more ornamentation, and more complexity while the underlying melodic skeleton remains recognizable.

### Score Analysis Methodology
Before playing a new piece:
1. Read through the jianpu without playing — identify structure, key changes, difficult passages
2. Identify the mode and key
3. Mark breathing points
4. Identify the 散慢中快散 structure (if applicable)
5. Listen to 2-3 different recordings by different performers
6. Note differences in interpretation between performers

## New Techniques

### Circular Breathing Refinement
- Now working toward musical application (not just exercise)
- Practice sustaining notes across phrase boundaries
- Apply to long held notes in southern-style pieces
- The goal: listeners should not be able to tell where you breathe
- Practice sequence: long tone → circular breath → continue same tone seamlessly

### Multi-Key Playing
- You should now own 2-3 dizis (D, G, and possibly C or E)
- Practice scales and exercises on each key
- Same fingerings, different pitches — but each instrument has a different "feel"
- Smaller bangdi requires tighter embouchure, less air
- Larger qudi requires wider embouchure, more air support
- Switching between instruments mid-practice session builds adaptability

### Half-Holing for Chromatic Notes
- Sliding finger to partially reveal a hole
- Creates notes between the standard scale degrees
- Essential for pieces that include b3, #4, b7, etc.
- Requires precise finger control and embouchure adjustment
- Practice: play standard note → slide to half-hole → back to standard, checking pitch with a tuner

### Harmonics (泛音)
- Special fingerings + specific embouchure to produce overtone pitches
- Extends the range beyond the standard 2.5 octaves
- Creates ethereal, floating tone quality
- Used sparingly for special effect
- Produced by overblowing while using a lower-octave fingering

### Advanced Ornament Combinations
- Combining 叠音 + 打音 in sequence
- Ornament patterns specific to regional styles
- Speed and precision refinement
- Northern style: sharp, percussive ornament clusters
- Southern style: smooth, connected grace note sequences

## Practice Routine (45 min/day)

1. Warm-up on current piece's dizi key (8 min)
2. Technical: scales in modes, chromatic exercises, circular breathing (10 min)
3. Ornament + articulation drills at speed (7 min)
4. Etude from CCOM exam book (5 min)
5. Repertoire — deep work on one piece (15 min)

## Songs in Jianpu

### Song 1: 牧民新歌 (Herdsman's New Song)
Grade 7 masterpiece by Jian Guangyi (简广易, 1966). The most famous dizi piece, depicting Mongolian herders' joy. Showcases virtually every dizi technique.

**Introduction — 散板**
```
牧民新歌 — Introduction
1=D  散板

0 0 0 3 5 | 6 - - - 5 6 | 1̇ - - - 6 1̇ |
2̇ - - - 1̇ 6 | 5 - - 3 5 6 | 5 3 2 - 1 2 |
3 - - - - - ||
```
Play freely. Sweeping and majestic, evoking vast grasslands. Each long note should have full, singing vibrato. The opening sets the scene — imagine standing on an endless plain under an open sky.

**Slow Section — 慢板**
```
1=D  4/4
Adagio ♩=52

3 - 5 3 | 2 - 1 2 | 3 - 5 6 | 5 - - - |
6 - 1̇ 6 | 5 - 3 5 | 6 - 5 3 | 2 - - - |
1 - 2 1 | 6̣ - 5̣ 6̣ | 1 - 2 3 | 2 - - - |
3 - 5 3 | 2 - 1 2 | 3 - 5 6 | 5 - - - ||
```
Rich vibrato on every held note. Singing, lyrical quality. This is a Mongolian long-song style melody — spacious and deeply felt. Let each phrase breathe naturally.

**Fast Section — 快板 (Double Tonguing)**
```
1=D  2/4
Vivace ♩=160

5 3 5 3 | 2 1 2 1 | 6̣ 1 6̣ 5̣ | 6̣ 1 2 3 |
5 3 5 3 | 2 1 2 1 | 6̣ 5̣ 6̣ 1 | 2 - |
5 6 5 3 | 2 3 2 1 | 6̣ 1 6̣ 5̣ | 6̣ 1 2 - |
3 5 3 2 | 1 2 1 6̣ | 5̣ 6̣ 5̣ 3̣ | 5̣ - ||
```
All double tongued (tu-ku-tu-ku). This is the signature fast section. Every note must be clean, even, and precise. Start slow (♩=100) and build speed gradually over weeks.

> **Listen:** [YouTube search: 牧民新歌 简广易 笛子](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=牧民新歌+简广易+笛子) | **Jianpu source:** [jianpu.cn](http://www.jianpu.cn/pu/30/306528.htm)

### Song 2: 春到湘江 (Spring Arrives at the Xiang River)
Grade 7-8 by Ning Baosheng (宁保生, 1976). A tour de force depicting the Xiang River in Hunan province awakening in spring. Requires circular breathing for the famous opening cadenza.

**Opening Cadenza**
```
春到湘江 — Opening Cadenza
1=D  散板

0 0 3 5 6 | 1̇ - - 6 5 | 6 1̇ 2̇ 1̇ 6 5 |
3 5 6 5 3 2 | 1 6̣ 5̣ 6̣ 1 2 | 3 - - - - |
```
The cadenza cascades like water rushing over rocks. Circular breathing allows the cascading runs to flow without interruption. Build intensity through the runs, then resolve to the held note.

**Lyrical Theme**
```
春到湘江 — Lyrical Theme
1=D  4/4
Andante ♩=66

6̣ 1 2 3 | 5 - 3 2 | 1 - 6̣ 1 | 2 - - - |
3 5 6 5 | 3 2 1 2 | 3 - 2 1 | 6̣ - - - |
5̣ 6̣ 1 2 | 3 - 5 3 | 2 - 1 6̣ | 5̣ - - - |
6̣ 1 2 3 | 5 - 6 5 | 3 - 2 1 | 2 - - - |
3 5 6 5 | 3 2 1 6̣ | 5̣ - 6̣ 1 | 6̣ - - - ||
```
The river theme — flowing, warm, graceful. Southern-style vibrato (气震音) throughout. Each phrase should feel like the gentle current of a great river. Ornament tastefully with grace notes and slides.

> **Listen:** [YouTube search: 春到湘江 笛子独奏](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=春到湘江+笛子独奏) | **Jianpu source:** [jianpuw.com](http://www.jianpuw.com/htm/gi/148208.htm)

### Song 3: 鹧鸪飞 (Partridge Flying)
Grade 6-7. A Hunan folk tune depicting partridges in flight. Multiple famous arrangements exist (Zhao Songting's version is most renowned). Southern style.

**Main Theme**
```
鹧鸪飞 — Main Theme
1=D  4/4
Adagio ♩=52

5 - - 6 | 1̇ - 6 5 | 3 - - 2 | 1 - - - |
5 - - 6 | 1̇ - 6 5 | 6 - - 5 | 3 - - - |
2 - - 3 | 5 - 3 2 | 1 - - 6̣ | 5̣ - - - |
6̣ - - 1 | 2 - 1 6̣ | 5̣ - - - | 0 - - - ||
```
Extreme legato. Long, singing phrases with rich vibrato and slides. The hallmark of southern style. Each note connects seamlessly to the next — no gaps, no breaks in the musical line. The partridge glides through the air without effort.

**Variation (Ornamented)**
```
鹧鸪飞 — Variation
1=D  4/4
Moderato ♩=60

5 3 5 - 6 | 1̇ 6 1̇ - 6 5 | 3 5 3 - 2 | 1 2 1 - - |
5 3 5 - 6 | 1̇ 6 1̇ - 6 5 | 6 5 6 - 5 | 3 5 3 - - |
2 1 2 - 3 | 5 3 5 - 3 2 | 1 6̣ 1 - 6̣ | 5̣ 6̣ 5̣ - - |
6̣ 5̣ 6̣ - 1 | 2 1 2 - 1 6̣ | 5̣ - - - | 0 - - - ||
```
The same melody, now ornamented. Each held note from the main theme is now embellished with grace notes and passing tones. This is the liu ban (六板) variation technique in action.

> **Listen:** [YouTube search: 鹧鸪飞 笛子独奏](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=鹧鸪飞+笛子独奏) | **Jianpu source:** [jianpu.cn](http://www.jianpu.cn/pu/48/48520.htm)

### Song 4: 五梆子 (Wu Bangzi / Five Beats)
A classic northern piece. Bright, energetic, uses the bangdi. Based on folk melodies from northern opera.

**Main Theme**
```
五梆子 — Main Theme
1=G  2/4
Allegro ♩=132

5 3 5 6 | 5 3 2 1 | 6̣ 1 2 3 | 2 1 6̣ 5̣ |
6̣ 1 2 1 | 6̣ 5̣ 3̣ 5̣ | 6̣ - | 0 0 |
5 6 1̇ 6 | 5 3 5 6 | 1̇ 6 5 3 | 2 1 6̣ 5̣ |
6̣ - | 0 0 ||
```
Bright, crisp bangdi sound. Northern tonguing style — sharp and percussive. Every note has a clear attack. This piece is all about rhythmic drive and energy.

**Development Section**
```
五梆子 — Development
1=G  2/4
Allegro ♩=140

5 3 5 3 | 5 6 5 3 | 2 1 2 1 | 2 3 2 1 |
6̣ 5̣ 6̣ 1 | 2 3 5 6 | 5 3 2 1 | 6̣ - |
5̣ 6̣ 1 2 | 3 5 6 5 | 3 2 1 6̣ | 5̣ 6̣ 1 2 |
3 2 1 6̣ | 5̣ 3̣ 5̣ 6̣ | 1 - | 0 0 ||
```
Increased speed and complexity. The development pushes the melody through different registers with rapid scalar passages.

> **Listen:** [YouTube search: 五梆子 笛子独奏](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=五梆子+笛子独奏) | **Jianpu source:** [sin80.com](https://www.sin80.com/score/2b5ecc5b)

### Song 5: 秋湖月夜 (Autumn Lake, Moonlit Night)
Grade 6 by Yu Xunfa (俞逊发). An atmospheric, introspective piece. Showcases tone color, dynamics, and rubato. Modern classic.

**Opening**
```
秋湖月夜 — Opening
1=D  散板

0 0 0 5̣ | 6̣ - - 1 | 2 - - 3 2 | 1 - - 6̣ 5̣ |
6̣ - - - | 0 0 0 0 |
```
From silence, the first note emerges like moonlight on water. Pianissimo. Let the silence between phrases speak.

**Main Theme**
```
秋湖月夜 — Main Theme
1=D  4/4
Adagio ♩=48

5̣ - 6̣ - | 1 - 2 1 | 6̣ - - 5̣ | 6̣ - - - |
1 - 2 - | 3 - 5 3 | 2 - 1 6̣ | 5̣ - - - |
6̣ - 1 - | 2 - 3 2 | 1 - 6̣ 5̣ | 6̣ - - - |
1 - 2 3 | 5 - 3 2 | 1 - 6̣ - | 5̣ - - - |
6̣ - - 1 | 2 - - 3 | 2 - 1 6̣ | 5̣ - - - ||
```
Extremely slow, meditative. Each note is a world of its own. Dynamic range from ppp to mp — never loud. The tone should be pure, clear, like moonlight reflected in still water. This is a piece about atmosphere and yi jing (意境) — artistic conception.

> **Listen:** [YouTube search: 秋湖月夜 俞逊发 笛子](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=秋湖月夜+俞逊发+笛子) | **Jianpu source:** [jianpu.cn](http://www.jianpu.cn/pu/84/84900.htm)

| Ma Di (马迪) | `马迪 笛子` | Northern | Composed 秦川抒怀 |

**Listen analytically:** Don't just enjoy — study HOW they play. Where do they breathe? How do they ornament? How do they shape phrases? Compare different performers playing the same piece. Notice how the same melody sounds completely different in different hands.

### Song 6: 凉凉 (Lương Lương / Cold Cold)

Theme of "三生三世十里桃花" (Eternal Love, 2017), by Zhang Bichen and Aska Yang. Modern Chinese-style ballad with wide range and dramatic dynamics. Hugely popular on Vietnamese sáo channels.

### Song 7: 菊花台 (Cúc Hoa Đài / Chrysanthemum Terrace)

Jay Chou (周杰伦), 2006, from the film "满城尽带黄金甲" (Curse of the Golden Flower). The quintessential 中国风 (Chinese-style) pop song. Beautiful on dizi — the song was composed with Chinese instruments in mind.

```
1=D  4/4

0 0 0 6̣ | 1 - 1 2 | 1 - 6̣ 5̣ | 6̣ - - - |
3 - 5 6 | 1̇ - 6 5 | 6 - 5 3 | 5 - - - |
3 - 5 6 | 1̇ - 6 5 | 3 - 2 1 | 2 - - - |
6̣ - 1 2 | 3 - 2 1 | 6̣ - 5̣ 6̣ | 1 - - - ||
```

### Song 8: 半壶纱 (Bán Hồ Sa / Half Pot of Gauze)

By Liu Ke Yi (刘珂矣), 2015. Chinese-style meditation music (禅意中国风). Extremely popular among Vietnamese sáo players — the flowing, meditative quality suits bamboo flute perfectly.

```
半壶纱 (Half Pot of Gauze)
1=D  4/4
Adagio ♩=66

0 0 5̣ 6̣ | 1 - 2 3 | 2 - 1 6̣ | 1 - - - |
0 0 6̣ 1 | 2 - 3 5 | 3 - 2 1 | 2 - - - |
0 0 2 3 | 5 - 6 5 | 3 - 2 3 | 5 - - - |
6 - 5 3 | 2 - 1 2 | 3 - 2 1 | 6̣ - - - |
5̣ - 6̣ 1 | 2 - 3 2 | 1 - 6̣ 5̣ | 6̣ - - - |
1 - 2 3 | 5 - 3 2 | 1 - 6̣ 1 | 2 - 1 6̣ |
5̣ - - - | 0 - - - ||
```
Play with extreme gentleness. Like incense smoke rising — unhurried, floating.

> **Listen:** [YouTube search: 半壶纱 笛子](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=半壶纱+笛子) | **Jianpu source:** [dizijun.com](http://www.dizijun.com/jianpu/153.html)
